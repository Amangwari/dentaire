1. Routine Dental Care

Routine dental care forms the foundation of a healthy smile. Our comprehensive services prevent, diagnose, and treat common dental conditions. From simple routine cleaning and decay prevention to serious conditions like root canals or extractions, our expertise addresses them all. We also take special care in managing dental anxiety so that patients with sensitive teeth undergo a totally pain-free experience.

Benefits

Preventive Focus: Regular checkups and cleaning help prevent dental problems, such as cavities and gum diseases, before they occur or progress.

Improved Oral Health: Regular care assists in long-term oral hygiene and reduces future dental-related problems.

Comforting and Caring: Our staff keeps patient comfort at the forefront, particularly for the anxious patients, in order to make all visits as comfortable as possible.

Personalized Treatment: Simple cleaning to complex treatments involving a root canal-whatever the case may be-are treated according to the patient's needs.

Patient Information

It is advisable to visit the dentist at least twice a year for regular checkups. When there are signs of tooth decay, sensitivity, or discomfort, early treatment helps avoid more serious concerns later on.

FAQs

We recommend a professional cleaning every six months to prevent plaque accumulation and/or to catch early signs of oral health issues. However, if you have certain conditions, such as gum disease, you may need cleanings more frequently.

A general dental check-up typically consists of a thorough check-up of teeth and gums along with an examination of the oral cavity. After that, professional cleaning is done, and X-rays can be taken to reveal developing problems not otherwise visible to the naked eye.

Health Tips

Brushing Techniques

Use a soft-bristled toothbrush and fluoride toothpaste to brush your teeth at least for two minutes, twice a day. Pay close attention to brushing every corner, including behind your back teeth.

Flossing Tips

Try to floss daily, as this removes plaque between the teeth and beneath the gumline. Be very gentle, gliding it against your teeth in a "C" shape around your tooth.

Dietary Recommendations

Keep sugar intake low and include calcium-rich food in your diet to make your teeth strong. Also, avoid frequent snacking to lower the chances of tooth decay.

3. Orthodontics

Our orthodontic treatment options are aimed at realigning deformed teeth and jaws into a healthier and more beautiful smile. We also provide traditional braces, clear aligners such as Invisalign, and preventive treatments for younger patients. Whether you want a discreet solution with clear aligners or require standard metal braces, our orthodontists have something to offer everyone, regardless of age.

Benefits

Improved Oral Health: Straightened teeth are easier to clean, reducing the risk of cavities, gum disease, and other dental problems.

Improved Aesthetics: Orthodontic treatment improves your smile by aligning misaligned teeth and thus providing a better facial symmetry.

Personalized Solutions: From metal braces to invisible aligners, we have everything to cater to every comfort level and lifestyle.

Long-term benefits: Properly aligned teeth can provide better chewing, speaking, and general oral functioning, which help in long-term benefits.

Patient Information

The duration of orthodontic treatments may vary from one case to another, but generally, most treatments may run for 12 to 24 months. Clear aligners provide the flexibility and discretion sought by many adult patients, while for other, more difficult cases, traditional braces may be more appropriate. Early intervention for children, at roughly age 7, is recommended as a guide for proper dental development.

4. Pediatric Dental Care

We understand that the dental needs of children are different from those of adults. Our pediatric dentistry services are designed to make each visit comfortable and entertaining so that your child learns healthy habits right from the beginning. Our team has experts in preventive care, prevention of dental decay, root canal treatment for milk teeth, and habits correction. We also provide sports mouth guards and counseling for parents to maintain the oral health of the child.

Benefits

Friendly Environment for Kids: The setting at our clinic is meant to provide a welcoming, non-threatening experience for children.

Preventive Care: Early dental check-ups prevent cavities, among other emerging dental issues.

Specialized Treatments: We provide targeted care ranging from crowns for milk teeth to habit correction and deal with the unique needs of children's dentistry.

Parental Education: We offer parental counseling to ensure you are equipped with the right knowledge to maintain your child’s oral health at home.

Patient Information

Children should see a dentist for the first time by age one, or six months after the first tooth erupts. Regular follow-up visits should occur every six months. Early dental visits can help a child become accustomed to the dentist and let the dentist begin preventive care such as fluoride treatments and sealants that can greatly reduce the risk of decay.

5. Teeth Replacement

Our options for missing teeth offer functional and aesthetic services: from dental implants to fixed crowns and bridges, to dentures-we provide every patient with choices according to one's lifestyle and dental needs. Dental implants can provide one with a permanent, natural appearance, while bridges and dentures allow restoration of the smile and the ability to chew and speak confidently.

Benefits

Restored Functionality: Teeth replacements help restore chewing, speaking, and facial structure.

Permanent Solutions: Dental implants are designed to last and provide stability, thus making them a permanent tooth replacement option.

Improved Aesthetics: Be it through implants, crowns, or dentures, our treatment options offer a more natural, visually pleasing smile.

Increased Confidence: Replacing missing teeth can be a great contribution to your self-esteem, and enrich the quality of life.

Patient Information

Options like teeth replacement vary in cost, time of procedure, and maintenance thereafter. Dental implants require minor surgery and complete integration in a couple of months' time, while bridges and dentures are less invasive but potentially may require periodic adjustments. Our team will guide you through the process to find the best solution for your specific needs.

Recovery Tips

After your new dental implant or bridge, some expected swelling and discomfort can occur. For the first few days, we recommend that you avoid hot beverages and a soft-food diet. Rinsing your mouth with warm salt water can help reduce swelling. Our team will give you detailed after-care instructions guaranteed to ensure an easy and fast recovery.

Long-Term Maintenance Advice

Paying close attention to your dental implants or fixed bridges will help ensure long-term success. Brush and floss as you normally would, with special attention around the implant area, and come see us every six months for your professional cleanings. For a denture: clean it daily, and let it soak in water or denture-cleaning solution at night to keep it shaped and fitted.

6. Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

We know you might have some questions before visiting the Dental Centre, and we are happy to give you the answers. Below, we have categorized our most frequently asked questions so that you can find information quickly.

Appointments & Scheduling

Q: How do I make an appointment at Dental Centre?

A: You can reach us on 011 4967 4967 for an appointment, use our online appointment booking facility, or visit the clinic in person.

Q: Do you take same-day appointments?

A: Yes, we accept same-day dental appointments. You can book an appointment anytime. However, we prefer you check the availability early in the day.

Q: What should I bring to my first appointment?

A: Please bring your insurance card if it’s applicable, and any old dental records or X-rays. If you are on medications, bring a list of those as well.

Dental Procedures

Q: What should I expect during my first visit?

A: Initial visits consist of an examination, cleaning, and review of your current dental health. We will also review any necessary X-rays and discuss treatment options with you if needed.

Q: Do root canals hurt?

A: Generally, root canal treatments are not painful, since we use local anesthesia to numb it. During the healing process, you may feel slight pain, but it is much less than the pain caused by the infection itself.

Q: How often should I visit the dentist for checkups?

A: We highly recommend regular cleanings and check-ups every six months for healthy teeth and mouth.

Q: Can you fix crooked teeth without braces?

A: Yes, we have clear aligners like Invisalign. Invisalign is discreet and quite effective at straightening teeth, basically a more cosmetic alternative to traditional braces.

Cosmetic Dentistry

Q: What is a smile makeover?

A: A smile makeover is a compilation of teeth whitening, veneers, crowns, and so on, which enhances the looks of your smile. Each individual treatment plan is distinctive to you and your personal needs and wants.

Q: How long does teeth whitening last?

A: Professional teeth whitening can last anywhere from six months to two years, depending on your oral hygiene habits and your daily life, whether it includes diet and smoking.

Q: Are dental veneers permanent?

A: Veneers will last for a period of ten to fifteen years provided proper care is managed. In most cases, they need to be replaced after they wear and tear over time.

Insurance & Payment Options

Q: Does Dental Centre accept insurance?

A: Yes, we do accept most major dental insurance plans. Please contact our office or check with your insurance provider to verify if we are in-network.

Q: What if I don’t have dental insurance?

A: We provide flexible payment plans and financing options for patients who have no insurance so that quality dental care is afforded by everyone.

Q: Do you offer payment plans for more expensive treatments?

A: Of course, we do have different payment plans and financing options available through our trusted partners to help spread out treatment costs easily.

Q: What forms of payment do you accept?

A: We accept cash, credit cards, debit cards, and other forms of payments through digital platforms. Payment plans are an option in some procedures.

Pediatric Dentistry

Q: When should I bring my child for their first dental visit?

A: We recommend bringing your child for their first dental visit by the age of one or as soon as their first tooth appears. Early visits help establish good oral care habits and ensure proper tooth development.

Q: How do you manage children's anxiety during visits?

A: Our pediatric dentists use child-friendly approaches to make them at ease. We are also offering our sedation options for such highly anxious children or for complicated procedures of dental restoration.

Q: Does my child need braces?

A: Depending on the alignment of your child's teeth and their bite, our orthodontist will determine how their dental development is going and recommend braces or clear aligners if they feel it is necessary.

Teeth Replacement

Q: What are my options for replacing missing teeth?

A: We have various options available for teeth replacement, viz., dental implants, fixed bridges, and dentures. Our dentists will be in a better position to discuss the best option relative to your needs and lifestyle.

Q: Are dental implants safe?

A: Yes, dental implants are a safe and highly effective solution for missing teeth. They are made up of biocompatible materials and, when placed correctly, have a high success rate.

Orthodontics

Q: How long will I need to wear braces?

A: It depends on the extent of the misalignment, but most patients usually wear braces within a time period of every other month between 18 and 24 months. Clear aligners might bring shorter treatment times upon certain opportunities.

Q: Are clear aligners as effective as braces?

A: Yes, clear aligners of are very effective in the treatment of mild to moderate misalignments. Our orthodontist will determine whether aligners or traditional braces serve best for your case.
